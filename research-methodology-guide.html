Methodology

Research methodology guide for students

A clear way to connect your research question, approach, sample, tools, data collection, and limitations.

Begin with the research question

Your methodology should explain how your method helps answer the research question. If the question asks about experiences, qualitative methods may fit. If it asks about measurement or comparison, quantitative methods may fit.

Explain your research design

Describe whether the study is qualitative, quantitative, mixed, descriptive, exploratory, experimental, or case-based. Then explain why that design makes sense.

Describe sample and data collection

  • Who or what is studied?
  • How were participants, documents, or cases selected?
  • What tools are used: questionnaire, interview guide, observation, dataset, or document analysis?

Be honest about limitations

Limitations do not ruin a project. They show that you understand scope, access, time, sample size, and data quality realistically.
