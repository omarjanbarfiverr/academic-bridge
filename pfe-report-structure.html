PFE Guide

How to structure a PFE report step by step

A practical structure students can use to organize their own final-year project clearly and ethically.

Table of contentsIntroductionCore chaptersFormatting checklistWhen to ask for support

Start with the purpose of the project

A strong PFE report usually begins by explaining the context, problem, objectives, research questions, scope, and expected contribution. The reader should understand why the project matters before seeing technical details.

Use a clear chapter sequence

  • Introduction: context, problem, objectives, research questions, scope.
  • Literature review: concepts, theories, previous studies, and research gap.
  • Methodology: approach, tools, sampling, data collection, and limitations.
  • Results or implementation: findings, analysis, prototype, case study, or applied work.
  • Conclusion: key outcomes, recommendations, limits, and future work.

Check formatting before final review

Review headings, table of contents, page numbers, figures, captions, references, appendices, margins, and university template rules. Formatting problems can make a serious project feel rushed.

Use support responsibly

Ethical support can help you improve structure, formatting, citations, proofreading, and presentation clarity. It should not replace your own research, analysis, or institutional responsibilities.
